My Children App UX/UI Project

Understand how parents think and care about their children, My Children IS a platform for active, accessible protection: manage children's schedules, track where children are, and protect children remotely in case the accident is out of control.

Discover

In current open society, children are always surrounded by lurking dangers, making dealing with them more difficult than ever. Moreover, the fact that parents do not know where their children are, what they are doing, and being out of control creates more risk.

Define

My Children aims to empower the 20-to-40-year-old parents to protect their children remotely.

Using research in the discover phase, I can produce valuable insights and understandings about the problem and the needs of users.

Caring your child's health
MyChildren will help parents monitor and manage their children's health better. Along with the app, MyChildren Smartbands or any smartwatches will track your children's movements, and based on height, weight, gender, age and sometimes daily living habits to measure indicators such as consumed calories, heart rate, temperature, etc.

Therefore, we focus on designing a minimal interface, using data and charts to help parents quickly get a clearer picture of children's health and compare it with the old data. Besides, the MyChildren AI system will make more general judgments based on the results of children. We keep the interface intuitive and consistent, along with using gradient colors as a highlight for the app to attract users more interest.
Managing your child's schedules
Parents can observe what their children are doing.

With the flow, parents can easily create, and duplicate tasks or events and your child will see their schedule on the smart band screen. Parents also can set reminders for children to notify them and view reports after they came to the right place.

In these screens, I use cards and blocks to make the interface more concise, as well as make it easy for users to add and remove tasks by swiping left.

take away


My Children is a fantastic experience of exploring and practicing design principles in user interface design. Specifically, this was the first time I had the opportunity with an emerging UX/UI industry in Vietnam.

Moreover, I was able to explore interesting ways in which forward-thinking design choices from the very first interaction can lead to a significantly enhanced user experience that otherwise would not have been possible.

Finally, I was able to deeply consider information hierarchy when designing the homepage, in finding a balance between the content users are consuming and what they might want to consume (to promote discovery).
